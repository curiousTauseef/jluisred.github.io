We are in constant state of trying to studying the world around us. If we get to understand the different facts being happened, we could be able to predict, replicate or leverage on them. But for effectively unveiling the functioning of those facts, we need data about them. Fortunalely, data is just out there. Bad news is, sometimes this information is difficult to capture, complex to interpret, and too big to be fully processed.
Semantic and Big Data technologies can put machines to work on our side, so we can stop fearing the immensity of the data and get in love with it instead. The Web has revealed its strengths as a mine of real world objects and shared taxonomies that machines can leverage on to process information in a much faster, human-driven way, opening new posibilities to reason and ultimately making sense out of it.
I'm a PhD candidate at Eurecom Institute in France, in the last year before my thesis defense. I obtained with honors my Master's thesis in Computer Science at the University of Extremadura, in Spain. I have been involved in EU projects like LinkedTV, MediaMixer and NexGenTV, and my research interests include Semantic Web, Multimedia Technologies and Knowledge Representation focusing on the annotation of News Stories.
